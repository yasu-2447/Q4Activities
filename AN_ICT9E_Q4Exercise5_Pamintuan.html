Light Bulbs and how they function

The typical incandescent light bulb contains a thin wire (usually tungsten) called a filament that has a high electrical resistance. This filament gets very hot when an electric current passes through it. The intense temperature makes the filament glow brightly.

Ohm's Law and how to calculate for Current

Ohm's principal discovery was that the amount of electric current through a metal conductor in a circuit is directly proportional to the voltage impressed across it, for any given temperature. Ohm expressed his discovery in the form of a simple quation, describing how voltage, current, and resistance interrelate: Voltage(E) = Current(I)Resistance(R)

Electrical Testing Safety

  • Proper planning and preparation can increase safety during testing.
  • A risk assessment is required by NFPA 70E for electrical safety.
  • Stopping work to fetch tools can be hazardous.
  • Traffic in the area poses a significant hazard.
  • An Energized Electrical Work Permit (EEWP) is necessary for work with electrical systems.
  • Exemptions for an EEWP exist but all other requirements of Article 130 still apply.
  • Annex J contains an example EEWP that can be modified to fit a specific job.
  • Before testing, determine the purpose and necessary test instruments.
  • Determine if a safety backup is necessary and what PPE is required.
  • Understand the circuit voltage, arc flash boundary, and incident energy.
